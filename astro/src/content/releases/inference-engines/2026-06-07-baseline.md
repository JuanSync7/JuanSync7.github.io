This is the inaugural release of the Inference Engines tracker. From here on, every week brings a fresh sourcing pass — version bumps, new features, status changes, and any newcomers worth watching — published as a dated post like this one.

## The lineup

Nine engines, grouped into three tiers.

**Server / production**

- **vLLM** — v0.22.1 (Jun 5 2026). The broadest-ecosystem default: PagedAttention, the widest hardware coverage (NVIDIA, AMD, TPU, Trainium, Gaudi), and the backbone of most Kubernetes serving stacks. Recent work: DeepSeek V4 hardening with NVFP4 fused MoE, KV Offload + Hybrid Memory Allocator, and deprecation of Transformers v4.
- **SGLang** — v0.5.12.post1 (May 23 2026). The throughput leader for prefix-heavy traffic thanks to RadixAttention. Speculative Decoding V2 is now default; CUDA 13 + PyTorch 2.11 is the new build baseline.
- **TensorRT-LLM** — container 25.12. NVIDIA's max-performance engine: highest ceiling, steepest setup, deep Triton integration. New B200 support and NVFP4 GEMM for Llama/Mixtral.
- **LMDeploy** — v0.13.0 (May 12 2026). TurboMind makes it the throughput champion for quantized serving — 4-bit runs ~2.4x faster than FP16. Added Qwen3.5 4-bit and Anthropic-compatible endpoints.

**Local / edge**

- **llama.cpp** — rolling (b87xx). Landed its largest rewrite since 2023 in April 2026: ~2.1x throughput on 70B quantized, backend-agnostic tensor parallelism, 1-bit quantization, and a Qualcomm Hexagon NPU backend.
- **Ollama** — rolling (early June 2026). The easiest local on-ramp, now with a Windows desktop GUI, cloud fallback for oversized models, and structured outputs.
- **MLC-LLM** — rolling. Compile-once, deploy-anywhere via Apache TVM — including in-browser (WebLLM/WebGPU) and on-device mobile.
- **ExLlamaV3 + TabbyAPI** — rolling. Best quality-per-bit on consumer GPUs (EXL3), often first to support brand-new quantized releases.

**Legacy**

- **TGI** — archived (read-only) March 21 2026 at v3.3.5. Hugging Face now points users to vLLM or SGLang. Tracked here only to flag the migration.

## Benchmark snapshot

On H100, 2026 head-to-heads put **SGLang (~16,215 tok/s)** and **LMDeploy (~16,132 tok/s)** roughly 29% ahead of a fully-optimized **vLLM (~12,553 tok/s)**. At ~1M requests/day that gap is worth about $15k/month in GPU spend — but vLLM still wins on ecosystem breadth and hardware coverage.

## Starter watchlist

Three names being tracked but not yet promoted: **llm-d** (Kubernetes-native distributed vLLM serving), **NanoFlow** (research-grade intra-device parallelism), and **DeepSpeed-MII** (Microsoft's serving layer, currently behind the leaders). Any that prove significant will graduate into the main list.
